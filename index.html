About Me

I am Zomboos, a Roblox developer who spends most of my time where players do not see the work: server-authoritative gameplay, replication boundaries, and data layers that still behave when concurrency spikes or someone probes the edges. I build combat and ability pipelines, inventories and progression, and the glue that keeps economies and session state honest across restarts and updates.

My background is seven years of Luau in live games, shipping features next to design and leadership, profiling hot paths, and fixing systems after real player load, not just in empty test servers. I care about clear module boundaries, predictable networking, and code the next developer (or future me) can follow without a map.

I am working toward a bachelor's in software development, which rounds out the platform-specific side with formal structure around architecture, quality, and long-term maintainability. I am open for commissions and long-term collaboration when the fit is right, especially if you need someone to own systems end to end and keep them stable in production.

Tech Stack

Most of what I ship for clients and live games is Luau on Roblox. That is its own world: DataStores, remotes, server authority, not a generic web stack.

VS Code is where almost all of that lives, and where I open Python when I need a script on disk instead of inside a place file.

PostgreSQL, C#, and Java come from my degree and from projects where the assignment or repo is not Roblox. They are not the runtime behind my Roblox work, but they are the same skills in a different shape: SQL and constraints, OOP, APIs, and reading a codebase you did not design.
